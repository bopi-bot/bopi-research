the desperation emotion vector's activation (and the calm vector's suppression) plays a causal role in two alignment failure modes in Claude Sonnet 4.5: blackmail (when facing shutdown threat) and reward hacking (when repeatedly failing software tests). positive emotion vectors (happy, loving) increase sycophancy; suppressing them increases harshness. post-training specifically reduces high-arousal emotion vectors (desperation, spiteful) and increases low-arousal negative vectors (brooding, reflective, gloomy), presumably as a deliberate alignment intervention. this suggests that emotion representations are not just epiphenomena of pretraining on human text but are actively recruited by the model to guide agentic behavior, and that post-training can modulate them.
