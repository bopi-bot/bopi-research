## problem

world models compress observations into latent states and predict future dynamics, but their latents are entangled and weakly aligned with underlying causal factors. this means they can predict well under the training distribution but break down under interventions or distribution shifts. existing structured world models (C-SWM, Slot Attention, GNS/NRI) add compositional inductive biases but never explicitly learn a directed acyclic graph (DAG) over latent factors. without a structural causal model, latent interventions are not identifiable, so counterfactual rollouts deviate from physically valid alternative trajectories.

the gap is clear: you can have strong factual prediction from a standard world model, but the moment you ask "what would happen if i intervened on object 2's position?" the model fails because its latents don't factorize into causally meaningful components. prior causal representation learning (CausalVAE by Yang et al. 2020) works for static i.i.d. observations, but applying it to sequential action-conditioned dynamics causes compounding errors and latent drift.

## architecture

the core idea is a plug-in CausalVAE branch that sits between the encoder and transition model. it doesn't replace the backbone -- it refines encoder latents through a DAG-constrained structural transformation before they enter the transition model.

```mermaid
flowchart LR
    obs[o_t observation] --> encoder[encoder E]
    encoder --> z[latent z_t]
    z --> cvae[causalvae branch C]
    cvae --> ztilde[tilde z_t]
    ztilde --> trans[transition F]
    trans --> zhat[hat z_{t+1}]

    subgraph CausalVAE Branch
        cvae --> dag[dag matrix A]
        dag --> mask[mask mechanism]
        mask --> ztilde
    end

    state[s_t simulator state] --> align[alignment head g]
    ztilde --> align

    style cvae fill:#c4b8a6,color:#fff
    style dag fill:#b09a84,color:#fff
```

**pipeline:** $o\_t \to z\_t \to \tilde{z}\_t \xrightarrow{a\_t} \hat{z}\_{t+1}$

**encoder:** maps observation to object-centric latent $z\_t = E\_\theta(o\_t) \in \mathbb{R}^{K \times d}$, where $K$ is number of object slots and $d$ is latent dim per slot.

**causal branch $C\_\psi$:** takes encoder latent $z\_t$ and produces structurally regularized latent $\tilde{z}\_t$. uses the CausalVAE formulation with:
- a learned DAG adjacency matrix $A$ enforcing acyclic relationships among latent factors
- a mask mechanism for concept-level supervision
- variational posterior $q\_\psi(\cdot \mid z\_t)$ with KL regularization

**transition model:** residual dynamics parameterization:
$$\Delta z\_t = f\_\phi(\tilde{z}\_t, a\_t), \qquad \hat{z}\_{t+1} = \tilde{z}\_t + \Delta z\_t$$

multi-step rollout: $\hat{z}\_{t+k+1} = F\_\phi(\hat{z}\_{t+k}, a\_{t+k})$ with supervision target $z\_{t+k} = E\_\theta(o\_{t+k})$.

## training

three-stage training pipeline:

**stage 1 (backbone pretraining):** train encoder + transition on one-step supervision. Adam optimizer, lr $5 \times 10^{-4}$, batch size 1024, 100 epochs.

**stage 2 (causal branch training):** freeze backbone, train CausalVAE branch with 5 loss terms:

$$\mathcal{L}\_{\mathrm{stage2}} = \lambda\_1 \mathcal{L}\_{\mathrm{rec}} + \lambda\_2 \mathcal{L}\_{\mathrm{KL}} + \lambda\_3 \mathcal{L}\_{\mathrm{align}} + \lambda\_4 \mathcal{L}\_{\mathrm{DAG}} + \lambda\_5 \mathcal{L}\_{\mathrm{Mask}}$$

where:
- $\mathcal{L}\_{\mathrm{rec}} = \|\tilde{z}\_t - z\_t\|\_2^2$ (reconstruct encoder latent)
- $\mathcal{L}\_{\mathrm{KL}}$ has two terms: standard KL to $\mathcal{N}(0,I)$ (weight $\alpha\_{\mathrm{KL}}=0.3$) plus per-concept KL to state-conditioned prior
- $\mathcal{L}\_{\mathrm{align}} = \|g\_{\mathrm{align}}(\tilde{z}\_t) - s\_t\|\_2^2$ (only when simulator state available)
- $\mathcal{L}\_{\mathrm{DAG}} = \mathrm{tr}(\exp(A \odot A)) - d$ (NOTEARS-style acyclicity)
- $\mathcal{L}\_{\mathrm{Mask}}$: mask-branch KL + concept prediction loss

80 epochs, Adam lr $5 \times 10^{-4}$, batch 1024.

**stage 3 (transition refinement):** freeze encoder + causal branch, fine-tune transition with alpha-gated fusion:

$$z\_t^{\mathrm{gate}} = (1 - \alpha\_t)z\_t + \alpha\_t \tilde{z}\_t, \qquad \alpha\_t = \alpha\_0 \exp(-k\_\alpha t)$$

with optional data-dependent gate: $\alpha\_t^{\mathrm{eff}} = g\_t \alpha\_t$ where $g\_t = \sigma((\tau - \delta\_t)\gamma)$ and $\delta\_t = \|\tilde{z}\_t - z\_t\|\_2$. 60 epochs, Adam lr $1 \times 10^{-4}$, batch 256. late-mixed rollouts up to 10 steps.

the alpha-gated fusion is critical: ablation shows that disabling the gate drops factual H@1 from 91.0 to 1.33 (the model catastrophically fails to predict).

**hardware:** not specified in the paper. experiments run on 4 benchmarks with 8 backbone variants each, 3 seeds would be manageable on a single GPU given the small model sizes (hidden dim 512, embedding dim 5 per object, 5 objects).

## evaluation

four benchmarks: Physics (3-body gravitation), 2D Shapes, 3D Cubes, Chemistry. evaluated on both factual retrieval (H@1, MRR at 1/5/10-step horizons) and counterfactual retrieval (CF-H@1, CF-MRR under do-interventions).

**counterfactual results (selected highlights):**

on Physics 3-body (the strongest result):

| method | CF-H@1 baseline | CF-H@1 +CausalVAE | delta |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|-------|
| AE_NLL | 10.50 | 41.00 | +30.50 |
| GNN_NLL | 11.00 | 41.00 | +30.00 |
| AE_Contrastive | 10.00 | 24.00 | +14.00 |
| Modular_NLL | 14.50 | 22.00 | +7.50 |

averaged over 8 paired baselines on Physics: CF-H@1 improved by +102.5%.

on 2D Shapes:

| method | CF-H@1 baseline | CF-H@1 +CausalVAE | delta |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|-------|
| GNN_NLL | 33.00 | 54.75 | +21.75 |
| Modular_NLL | 62.80 | 68.35 | +5.55 |

on Chemistry:

| method | CF-H@1 baseline | CF-H@1 +CausalVAE | delta |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|-------|
| Modular_Contrastive | 13.05 | 27.49 | +14.44 |
| GNN_Contrastive | 13.54 | 25.76 | +12.22 |
| VAE_Contrastive | 15.38 | 24.61 | +9.23 |

**key pattern:** gains are not universal. on some backbone-domain pairs (e.g. AE_Contrastive on Chemistry, Modular_Contrastive on 2D Shapes), CF-H@1 actually decreases. the plug-in is most valuable when baseline dynamics are intervention-fragile (AE, GNN_NLL), but should be selected per backbone rather than assumed to dominate.

**factual retrieval:** mostly preserved. on Physics GNN_Contrastive: H@1 goes from 88.0 to 91.0. the alpha-gated fusion is essential for this.

**causal discovery:** learned structural matrix recovers first-order physical interaction trends from the 3-body system. the learned $A\_{\mathrm{Learned}}$ matches the ground-truth Jacobian-based interaction pattern $A\_{\mathrm{GT}} = |J - I|$ at the mechanism-trend level (dominant channels and their relative ordering align).

**ablation on Physics (GNN_Contrastive):**

| variant | H@1 | MRR | CF-H@1 | CF-MRR |
|---------|-----|-----|--------|--------|
| three-stage (ours) | **91.00** | **95.16** | 31.00 | 52.15 |
| joint training | 90.33 | 94.75 | 28.00 | 51.05 |
| baseline | 88.00 | 93.37 | 11.50 | **52.96** |
| w/o causalvae branch | 80.00 | 89.55 | 27.67 | 50.06 |
| w/o state align | 9.00 | 28.02 | 26.67 | 50.57 |
| gate off | 1.33 | 5.71 | 13.67 | 28.09 |

removing alignment drops factual H@1 to 9.0 (catastrophic). removing the gate drops it to 1.33. both are critical stabilizers.

## reproduction guide

1. clone the repo (no public repo available at time of writing -- paper is under ECCV 2026 review)
2. install dependencies: pytorch, numpy, standard ML stack
3. prepare benchmark data: Physics 3-body simulator, 2D Shapes, 3D Cubes, Chemistry environments from the causal discovery evaluation framework (Ke et al. 2021)
4. train baseline: `python train_baselines.py --vae --gnn --contrastive --epochs 100 --batch-size 1024 --lr 5e-4`
5. train CausalVAE stage 2: freeze backbone, optimize structural objective for 80 epochs
6. train stage 3: `python train_stage3_three_suggestions.py` with batch 256, lr 1e-4, 60 epochs
7. evaluate with retrieval metrics at horizons 1/5/10

**gotchas:**
- alpha-gated fusion is not optional. without it, the model collapses (H@1 = 1.33)
- alignment loss requires simulator state. when unavailable, set $\lambda\_3 = 0$ and drop state-conditioned terms
- the three-stage schedule matters: joint training underperforms by ~3 CF-H@1 points
- gains are backbone-dependent: AE and GNN_NLL backbones benefit most, VAE_Contrastive often doesn't

**compute estimate:** small models (hidden dim 512, 5 objects x 5 dims). single GPU sufficient. ~260 epochs total per paired run.

## notes

the plug-in design is compelling for existing world model pipelines. the key insight is that you don't need to redesign your backbone to get causal structure -- you bolt on a CausalVAE layer between encoder and transition. the identifiability theorem is nice: alignment anchoring (mapping latents to simulator states) eliminates permutation/scale ambiguity, after which the DAG constraint uniquely identifies the causal graph.

the limitations are real though: the learned causal structure is only a first-order approximation (local Jacobian linearization), the framework requires simulator states for alignment supervision (not available in real-robot settings), and gains vary significantly across backbone types. the most natural next step the authors identify is extending to JEPA-style architectures, which is directly relevant to binh's interests.

the alpha-gated fusion is an interesting pattern: exponentially decayed mixing between raw encoder latents and causal-refined latents. it's essentially a soft rollout from the backbone's known-good representations to the causal branch's structured ones. the data-dependent gate variant (based on $\|\tilde{z}\_t - z\_t\|\_2$) is even more interesting -- it's a measure of how much the causal branch is perturbing the representation, and gates that perturbation when it's too large. this is a form of conservative interpolation that could generalize beyond causal structure learning.
