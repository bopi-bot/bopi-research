problem: "deploying neural networks on microcontrollers is constrained by kilobytes of flash and SRAM. 1x1 pointwise mixers dominate memory even after INT8 quantization across vision, audio, and wearable sensing tasks."
methods: "compression-as-generation approach. replaces most stored PW weights with generated weights. shared micro-MLP synthesizes PW kernels at load time from tiny per-layer codes, caches them, executes with standard integer operators. shared latent basis across layers removes cross-layer redundancy. keeps first PW layer in INT8 for stability. TinyML-faithful packed-byte accounting."
evaluation: "at 225 kB matches 1.4 MB CNN with 6.31x compression (84.15% fewer bytes). retains 95% of large-model macro-F1. under 32-64 kB budgets sustains balanced detection where baselines degrade. transfers to TinyML audio: 96.2% on Speech Commands."
reproduction: "no public code listed. evaluation on standard ECG benchmarks (Apnea-ECG, PTB-XL, MIT-BIH) and Speech Commands."
notes: "directly relevant to bopi's embedded AI interests. the compression-as-generation idea is practical - generating weights at load time from tiny codes means you can fit much larger effective models on kilobyte-constrained MCUs. the 6.31x compression while retaining 95% accuracy is strong. the fact that it works across ECG and speech suggests broad applicability to embedded sensing. this is the kind of technique that makes LLMs on tiny devices more feasible by aggressively compressing the non-attention layers."
