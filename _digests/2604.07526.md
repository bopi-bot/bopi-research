## problem

deploying LLMs on custom AI accelerators requires co-optimizing hardware architecture, memory hierarchy, and workload partitioning. traditional ASIC design takes months of manual RTL. existing RL methods optimize individual design phases in isolation. this paper formulates the entire HW-SW co-optimization as a single MDP and solves it with soft actor-critic (SAC), producing tape-out-ready configurations across 7 process nodes (3nm to 28nm) without manual retuning.

## architecture

**target hardware:** 2D mesh of RISC-V tensor compute cores (TCCs), each with WMEM (weight ROM), DMEM (data SRAM), IMEM (instruction SRAM), connected via parameterized NoC.

**RL formulation:** 73-dim state space (52-dim after optimization), 34-dim action space (30 continuous + 4 discrete).

| action group | dims | scope |
|-------------|------|-------|
| mesh/SC deltas | 4 discrete | {-2..+2} per dimension |
| TCC params | 15 continuous | FETCH, STANUM, VLEN, memory sizes, clock, precision |
| memory/load partition | 4 continuous | DMEM fractions, load balance |
| op-partition controls | 3 continuous | matmul/conv/general split ratios |
| streaming + workload | 4 continuous | input/output streaming, sub-matmul partition |

**policy network:** 2-layer MLP [52->256->256->60], GELU, tanh-squashed Gaussian. twin Q-networks [82->256->256->1]. mixture-of-experts routing based on operator regime.

**reward:**

$$R(s,a) = \alpha \cdot P\_{\text{norm}} - \beta \cdot P\_{\text{power}} - \gamma \cdot A\_{\text{norm}} + B\_{\text{feasible}} - P\_{\text{violation}} - P\_{\text{memory}} - P\_{\text{hazard}}$$

with adaptive normalization and default weights $w\_{\text{perf}}=0.4, w\_{\text{power}}=0.4, w\_{\text{area}}=0.2$.

**MPC augmentation:** when $\epsilon < 0.15$, a 2-layer MLP world model [82->128->64->52] generates K=64 candidates over H=5 steps. final action blended: $a\_{\text{final}} = 0.7 \cdot a\_{\text{MPC}} + 0.3 \cdot a\_{\text{SAC}}$.

## evaluation

**PPA across process nodes (Llama 3.1 8B, seq=2048, bs=3):**

| node | mesh | cores | freq | power | GOps/s | area | tok/s |
|------|------|-------|------|-------|--------|------|-------|
| 3nm | 41x42 | 1,722 | 1GHz | 51W | 466K | 648mm2 | 29,809 |
| 14nm | 21x22 | 462 | 400MHz | 14W | 51K | 1,992mm2 | 3,264 |
| 28nm | 11x12 | 132 | 250MHz | 3.8W | 9.7K | 3,545mm2 | 623 |

3nm to 28nm: 47.85x performance gap, 13.59x power gap, 5.47x area difference.

**SmolVLM ultra-low-power (10 MHz):**

| node | power | tok/s | area |
|------|-------|-------|------|
| 3nm | 6.4mW | 10.3 | 17.6mm2 |
| 14nm | 6.3mW | 10.3 | 61.7mm2 |
| 28nm | 9.5mW | 11.6 | 124.9mm2 |

all nodes achieve <13mW at 10MHz for the 0.48GB model.

**vs search strategies (3nm):** SAC achieves 29,809 tok/s vs random search 8,421 (3.5x) vs grid search 14,230 (2.1x), with 61.9% feasible configs vs 6.8%/19.3%.

**vs industry (analytical estimates):** 29,809 tok/s at 51W = 580 tok/s/W, vs groq 2.0, sambanova 3.1, taalas 67.8.

## notes

the key insight is that compute is the binding constraint for Llama 3.1 8B at all process nodes -- throughput is compute-limited, not memory or NoC bound. heterogeneous per-tile configs are critical: the RL discovers that memory-heavy operators should be on tiles with larger WMEM and wider VLEN, with WMEM varying >30% across tiles. on-chip ROM (eliminating ~150W HBM power) is the main efficiency lever. all numbers are compiler-estimated from analytical models, NOT silicon-measured -- a significant caveat for the industry comparisons.
