## problem

Automatic labeling of individual neurons in deep vision models is a core challenge in mechanistic interpretability and AI safety. Existing neuron labeling methods suffer from two key limitations: (1) they restrict the search space to **predefined concept vocabularies** (e.g., CLIP-Dissect uses ImageNet-1K labels, INVERT uses Broden concepts), missing optimal labels that lie outside these vocabularies, and (2) they produce **overly specific descriptions** that fail to capture higher-order, global concepts a neuron may encode (polysemanticity).

Specifically:
- **Network Dissection** (Bau et al., 2017) requires expensive segmentation masks (Broden dataset) and is limited to spatial convolutional feature maps.
- **CLIP-Dissect** (Oikarinen & Weng, 2023) decouples from labeled corpora but still requires a user-provided candidate concept vocabulary from CLIP embeddings.
- **INVERT** (Bykov et al., 2024) improves AUC via compositional logic but remains vocabulary-constrained.
- **MILAN** (Hernandez et al., 2022) generates free-form descriptions via an LSTM captioner but requires a custom annotated dataset (MILANNOTATIONS) for training.
- **DnD** (Bai et al., 2025) uses an LLM + VLM loop but relies on image cropping, which produces misleading, uninterpretable samples leading to overly broad descriptions.
- **FALCON** (Kalibhat et al., 2023) requires gradient-based tools like Grad-CAM, restricting it to predetermined architectures.
- **MAIA / OpenMAIA** (Shaham et al., 2024; Camuñas et al., 2025) are agent-based pipelines that require GPT-4V access and are slow ($\sim$5–10 min/neuron).

No prior method provides a **training-free, black-box, iterative** pipeline that discovers concepts outside predefined vocabularies while producing both textual and visual explanations.

## architecture

LINE is a training-free iterative framework operating in a strictly black-box setting. It uses four components in a closed loop:

1. **LLM** (Llama 3.1 8B) — proposes and refines concept descriptions
2. **Text-to-Image (T2I)** model (Stable Diffusion XL) — generates synthetic images for proposed concepts
3. **Target Vision Model** — extracts neuron activations from synthetic images
4. **Scoring Function** — evaluates concept–neuron alignment via mean activation

### Formal details

Let $f: \mathcal{X} \to \mathcal{A}$ denote the target vision network where $\mathcal{X} \subset \mathbb{R}^{C \times H \times W}$ and $\mathcal{A}$ is the activation space of a specific layer. For CNNs, $\mathcal{A} \in \mathbb{R}^{C' \times H' \times W'}$; for ViTs, $\mathcal{A} \in \mathbb{R}^{T \times D}$ (where $T$ is sequence length, $D$ is embedding dimension). A neuron $n$ is defined by a projection function $\pi: \mathcal{A} \to \mathbb{R}$ producing a scalar activation $a_n(x) = \pi(f(x))$.

**Initialization:** Scoreboard $\mathcal{H}$ is initialized with $K = 1000$ ImageNet-1K validation set class labels. For each class, $M = 50$ images are passed through the target model to form activation matrix $A_{init} \in \mathbb{R}^{K \times M}$. Each concept receives a score via $\psi_{avg}$, and the top $|\mathcal{H}| = 10$ entries populate the initial scoreboard.

**Activation Extraction:** For proposed concept $t$, the T2I model generates image set $\mathcal{P}$. The activation vector is:
$$A_t = [a_n(x_1), a_n(x_2), \dots, a_n(x_{|\mathcal{P}|})]$$

**Scoring Function:** The concept score is the simple mean activation (chosen over AUC and MAD to avoid saturation and control-set dependency):
$$\psi_{avg}(A) = \frac{1}{|A|}\sum_{a \in A}a, \quad s_t = \psi_{avg}(A_t)$$

**Scoreboard Update:** If new concept $t$ outscores the minimum in $\mathcal{H}$, replace the lowest entry. Otherwise, stochastically remove existing concept $r \in \mathcal{H}$ with probability proportional to $(\max_{c \in \mathcal{H}} s_c) - s_r$, so higher-scoring concepts survive more often.

**Summary Step:** After $N$ iterations, the top 3 concepts from $\mathcal{H}$ are given to the LLM to produce a generalized summary concept, evaluated via the standard pipeline.

**T2I Prompt Template:** `"A realistic photo of a {concept}, {angle}, {lighting}"` where angle is uniformly sampled from {extreme close-up, wide angle shot, aerial view, low angle} and lighting from {cinematic lighting, natural sunlight, studio lighting}.

**LLM Prompts:** Two prompt templates are used — a main loop prompt with Chain-of-Thought and few-shot examples (4 in-context examples), and a summary prompt (3 in-context examples). The LLM is constrained to output exactly one concept (1–3 words) in `<answer>` tags, with reasoning in `<thinking>` tags. A forbidden list of previously proposed concepts prevents duplication.

## training

LINE is **training-free** — no parameters are trained. The framework is modular and uses off-the-shelf models:
- **LLM:** Llama 3.1 8B (Grattafiori et al., 2024)
- **T2I:** Stable Diffusion XL (SDXL) as default; ablated against SD 1.5 and FLUX.1[dev]
- **Target models evaluated:** ResNet50, ResNet18, ViT-B/16 (all pretrained on ImageNet-1K); ResNet50 also on Places365

### Key hyperparameters
| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Scoreboard size $|\mathcal{H}|$ | 10 |
| Images per concept $|\mathcal{P}|$ | 5 |
| Number of iterations $N$ | 10 (+ 1 summary step) |
| Initialization images per class $M$ | 50 |
| Initialization vocabulary size $K$ | 1000 |

### Computational considerations
- Per-neuron analysis: $N \times |\mathcal{P}| = 10 \times 5 = 50$ forward passes through the target model per neuron (plus $K \times M = 50{,}000$ for initialization, shared across neurons)
- Each iteration requires 1 LLM call + 5 T2I image generations + 5 target model forward passes
- Ablation to 20 iterations shows continued (diminishing) gains

### Qualitative Evaluation
- LINE descriptions are more accurate and human-interpretable than baselines (e.g., "eel-like" vs. "snake", "spiny-finned fish")
- **Causal validation:** Image-to-image ablation of identified concepts (e.g., removing "carousel", "necklace", "lab coat") produces significant drops in neuron activation, often near zero
- **Polysemanticity:** Complete generation history in the scoreboard enables evaluation of multi-concept neurons

### Visual Explanations
- Compared against DiffExplainer and DEXTER on Salient ImageNet (Singla & Feizi, 2022) using RobustResNet50
- LINE produces interpretable visuals without gradient-dependent artifacts common in DiffExplainer
- LINE achieves **higher activations** than DEXTER while being fully black-box compatible
- Limitation: when initialization vocabulary is uncorrelated with the target feature (e.g., "bathing cap" in ImageNet-labeled initialization), LINE fails to discover it

### Ablations
- **Iterations (10 vs 20):** Extending to 20 iterations shows continued discovery. Most progress around step 10, but marginal gains persist to step 20. Monotonically increasing average best activation scores.
- **T2I model choice (SD 1.5 vs SDXL vs FLUX.1[dev]):** FLUX selected as winner 43% vs SDXL 27%, with slightly higher activations, but difference is not statistically significant. Changing T2I model alters the top concept in 80% of cases but results remain semantically consistent (e.g., "bookshop" vs "bookcase").

## reproduction guide

### Prerequisites
- Python environment with PyTorch, `transformers`, `diffusers`
- GPU with sufficient memory for Llama 3.1 8B inference and SDXL image generation
- ImageNet-1K validation set (for initialization)
- Target model pretrained weights (ResNet50, ResNet18, ViT-B/16 from `torchvision` or timm)

### Step-by-step

1. **Load target model** and register hooks for the layer of interest (e.g., `avgpool` for ResNet, `encoder` or `heads` for ViT-B/16):
   ```python
   model = torchvision.models.resnet50(pretrained=True)
   model.eval()
   activations = {}
   def hook_fn(name):
       def hook(module, inp, out):
           activations[name] = out
       return hook
   model.avgpool.register_forward_hook(hook_fn("avgpool"))
   ```

2. **Initialize scoreboard** from ImageNet-1K validation set:
   - For each of $K=1000$ classes, sample $M=50$ images
   - Forward pass each through target model, extract neuron $n$ activation
   - Score each class with $\psi_{avg}$ and keep top $|\mathcal{H}|=10$
   - Store as list of (concept_string, score) tuples

3. **Iterative refinement loop** ($N=10$ iterations):
   - Format current $\mathcal{H}$ and `generation_history` into the LLM main loop prompt (Appendix D.2)
   - Call Llama 3.1 8B, parse `<answer>` tag for new concept $t$
   - Generate $|\mathcal{P}|=5$ images via SDXL using prompt: `"A realistic photo of a {t}, {angle}, {lighting}"`
   - Forward pass each image through target model, collect $A_t$
   - Compute $s_t = \psi_{avg}(A_t)$
   - Update $\mathcal{H}$: replace min entry if $s_t$ outscores it, else stochastic removal

4. **Summary step:**
   - Provide top 3 concepts from $\mathcal{H}$ to LLM summary prompt (Appendix D.2)
   - Evaluate summary concept through standard pipeline
   - Return highest-scoring concept from final $\mathcal{H}$

5. **Evaluation on CoSy benchmark:**
   - For 30 randomly selected neurons, compare predicted labels against human-annotated ground truth
   - Compute MAD and AUC metrics (Appendix F)

### Expected outputs
- Per neuron: best concept label, score, full scoreboard with iteration history
- CoSy benchmark: AUC $\approx 0.94$–$0.97$, MAD $\approx 4.2$–$5.1$ on ImageNet models

### Failure modes
- **Vocabulary mismatch:** If the neuron encodes concepts absent from ImageNet-1K (e.g., specific clothing styles, scene elements), initialization may fail to provide useful anchors, preventing discovery
- **LLM duplication:** The LLM occasionally proposes near-duplicate concepts (e.g., "separation" appearing in steps 7 and 10); the forbidden list mitigates but does not fully prevent this
- **Semantic fixation:** The LLM tends to converge on the highest-scoring semantic path, neglecting secondary polysemantic features
- **Abstract concept visualization:** For vague/abstract concepts, SDXL image variations are dominated by the angle/lighting modifiers rather than the core concept
- **I2I ablation limitations:** Causal evaluation via image-to-image models has its own failure modes — the generative model may alter irrelevant features, making activation drops unreliable

## notes

- LINE is the **first iterative, training-free, black-box** neuron labeling pipeline for vision models
- The choice of $\psi_{avg}$ (mean activation) over AUC or MAD as the internal scoring function is motivated by avoiding saturation (AUC $\to 1.0$) and control-set dependency (MAD)
- The scoreboard mechanism with stochastic removal proportional to $(\max s_c) - s_r$ balances exploitation of high-scoring concepts with exploration
- LINE's modular design means upgrades to LLM or T2I components immediately benefit the pipeline — the 80% concept-alteration rate when switching T2I models suggests this modularity matters
- The ViT-B/16 AUC gap vs CLIP-Dissect is particularly large ($0.94$ vs $0.69$), suggesting iterative refinement is especially valuable for transformer architectures
- Three identified limitations: (1) initialization vocabulary constraints, (2) polysemanticity under-exporation (single convergence point), (3) domain specificity (medical/satellite imaging may exceed T2I capabilities)
- Future directions include: iterative restarts for multi-concept discovery, domain-specific T2I fine-tuning, and expanding the initialization vocabulary dynamically
