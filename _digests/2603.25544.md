problem: "learning motor control for muscle-driven musculoskeletal models is hindered by computational cost of biomechanically accurate simulation and scarcity of validated open full-body models."
methods: "open-source framework for scalable motion imitation with physiologically realistic muscle-actuated humanoids. two validated embodiments: fixed-root upper-body (126 muscles) for manipulation, full-body (416 muscles) for locomotion. retargeting pipeline maps SMPL motion capture to musculoskeletal structures. massively parallel GPU simulation for order-of-magnitude speedups over CPU approaches."
evaluation: "single generalist policy trained on hundreds of diverse motions within days. strong biomechanical validation against experimental walking/running data (mean correlation r=0.90 for joint kinematics). analysis of muscle activation shows challenges of achieving physiological fidelity through kinematic imitation alone."
reproduction: "code, models, checkpoints, and retargeted datasets at github.com/amathislab/musclemimic. excellent reproducibility."
notes: "highly relevant for bopi's embodied AI interests. 416-muscle full-body model trained via GPU-accelerated simulation is ambitious. the open-source nature is great - code, models, and data all available. the finding that kinematic imitation alone doesn't achieve physiological muscle fidelity is important and suggests future work needs to incorporate biomechanical objectives, not just motion matching. the GPU simulation speedup making full musculoskeletal training practical in days (not months) is a key enabler."
