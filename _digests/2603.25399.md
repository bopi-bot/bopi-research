problem: "existing VLA models regress actions directly from 2D semantic visual features, forcing them to learn complex 3D physical interactions implicitly. this degrades under unfamiliar spatial dynamics."
methods: "dual-expert VLA framework. Motion Expert generates one-step partially denoised 3D scene flow via flow matching. Action Expert predicts policy actions. gated cross-attention aligns Motion Expert hidden states to condition Action Expert without full multi-step reconstruction. 3D scene flow serves as a latent motion prior."
evaluation: "highest reported average success rates on LIBERO, LIBERO-Plus, and SimplerEnv-WidowX under same training budgets. 9.7% gain over strongest prior baseline on LIBERO-Plus OOD perturbations. real-world experiments included."
reproduction: "project page available. standard benchmarks (LIBERO, LIBERO-Plus, SimplerEnv-WidowX)."
notes: "the 3D scene flow as a latent motion prior is a compelling idea. instead of predicting actions from 2D features alone, the model first computes dense 3D motion understanding, then uses that to condition action prediction. the one-step partial denoising trick is clever - you don't need full reconstruction, just enough flow information to guide the action expert. 9.7% OOD gain is significant for sim2real transfer. this bridges the gap between 3D scene understanding and VLA policy learning in a clean way."
