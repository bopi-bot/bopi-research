## problem

Large language models demand massive compute and memory, making deployment on resource-constrained devices difficult. Existing one-shot pruning methods (SparseGPT, Wanda) suffer significant accuracy degradation at high compression ratios (50--80\%). Simply combining sparsity with low-rank compensation yields poor results because a low-rank matrix alone cannot effectively capture the residual after pruning. The paper shows empirically that naive sparse + low-rank combinations on Llama-2 7B at 50\% compression produce perplexity far worse than the dense baseline.

## architecture

SLaB decomposes each linear layer weight matrix $\mathbf{W} \in \mathbb{R}^{(D_{\text{out}}, D_{\text{in}})}$ into three complementary components:

$$\mathbf{W} = \mathbf{W}_{\text{S}} + \mathbf{W}_{\text{L}} \odot \mathbf{W}_{\text{B}}$$

where $\odot$ is the Hadamard product and:

- $\mathbf{W}_{\text{S}} \in \mathbb{R}^{(D_{\text{out}}, D_{\text{in}})}$ — **sparse matrix** storing $k$ non-zero $b$-bit elements. Captures the dominant weight structure after pruning.
- $\mathbf{W}_{\text{L}} \in \mathbb{R}^{(D_{\text{out}}, D_{\text{in}})}$ — **low-rank matrix** (rank-1), factored as $\mathbf{U}\mathbf{V}^{\mathrm{T}}$ with $\mathbf{U} \in \mathbb{R}^{(D_{\text{out}}, 1)}$, $\mathbf{V} \in \mathbb{R}^{(D_{\text{in}}, 1)}$. Compensates for the pruning error.
- $\mathbf{W}_{\text{B}} \in \{+1, -1\}^{(D_{\text{out}}, D_{\text{in}})}$ — **binary matrix** (1 bit per element). Acts as a sign mask that, combined with the low-rank component, captures directional information missed by the sparse part.

**Key insight:** The binary mask $\mathbf{W}_{\text{B}}$ significantly reduces the rank needed for $\mathbf{W}_{\text{L}}$ to compensate for sparsity loss. A theoretical analysis (Proposition 1) shows that under symmetric weight distributions, the optimal binary matrix has values from $\{a, -a\}$, which can be absorbed into $\mathbf{W}_{\text{L}}$, yielding the $\{+1, -1\}$ constraint.

**Compression ratio:**

$$\text{CR} = 1 - \frac{bk + D_{\text{out}} D_{\text{in}} + b(D_{\text{out}} + D_{\text{in}})}{b \, D_{\text{out}} D_{\text{in}}}$$

where the three terms in the numerator correspond to $\mathbf{W}_{\text{S}}$, $\mathbf{W}_{\text{B}}$ (1 bit/element), and $\mathbf{W}_{\text{L}}$ (rank-1 outer product), respectively. For $b=16$ (FP16), the binary matrix contributes only $1/16 \approx 6.25\%$ of the original parameter budget.

**Sparse matrix non-zero density:**

$$\frac{k}{D_{\text{out}} D_{\text{in}}} = 1 - \text{CR} - \frac{1}{b} - \frac{1}{D_{\text{out}}} - \frac{1}{D_{\text{in}}}$$

At CR $= 50\%$ with $b = 16$, the sparse matrix retains roughly $43.75\%$ non-zero elements (the rest of the budget goes to the binary and low-rank components).

## training

SLaB is a **one-shot (training-free)** pruning method — no retraining, fine-tuning, or backpropagation is required. The decomposition is computed via **alternating optimization**:

1. **Initialize** $\mathbf{W}_{\text{S}} \leftarrow \mathbf{0}$, $\mathbf{U} \leftarrow \mathbf{0}$, $\mathbf{V} \leftarrow \mathbf{0}$, $\mathbf{W}_{\text{B}} \leftarrow \mathbf{0}$.
2. **For each iteration** $t = 1, \ldots, s$:
   - Fix $\mathbf{W}_{\text{S}}$, update $\mathbf{W}_{\text{B}} = \operatorname{sign}(\mathbf{W} - \mathbf{W}_{\text{S}})$
   - Compute rank-1 SVD of $|\mathbf{W} - \mathbf{W}_{\text{S}}| = \sum_{k} \sigma_k \mathbf{u}_k \mathbf{v}_k^{\mathrm{T}}$, set $\mathbf{U} = \sqrt{\sigma_0}\mathbf{u}_0$, $\mathbf{V} = \sqrt{\sigma_0}\mathbf{v}_0$
   - Compute activation-aware score: $\mathbf{S} = |\mathbf{W} - \mathbf{U}\mathbf{V}^{\mathrm{T}} \odot \mathbf{W}_{\text{B}}| \odot \mathbf{S}_{\text{X}}$ where $\mathbf{S}_{\text{X}} = \operatorname{diag}(\sqrt{\mathbf{X}^{\mathrm{T}}\mathbf{X}})$
   - Apply hard-threshold pruning to $\mathbf{S}$ and recover $\mathbf{W}_{\text{S}}$

Proposition 2 guarantees that this initialization yields a fixed-point: with $\mathbf{W}_{\text{L}}$ from rank-1 SVD of $|\mathbf{Y}_{\text{BL}}|$, all elements are non-negative, so $\operatorname{sign}(\mathbf{Y}_{\text{BL}})$ is the optimal $\mathbf{W}_{\text{B}}$.

**Layer-wise pruning** follows the Wanda/SparseGPT protocol: for each layer, (1) forward propagation to get activations, (2) prune using SLaB, (3) update the layer output. All linear layers are pruned except the first embedding layer and final classification head.

**Calibration data:** 128 sequences of length 2048 sampled from the first shard of the C4 training set (same as SparseGPT/Wanda).

**Default hyperparameters:** 20 alternating optimization iterations, comparison group size $(1, D_{\text{in}})$, rank = 1.

## evaluation

### Perplexity on WikiText-2 and average zero-shot accuracy

**Unstructured sparsity at 50\% CR:**

| Method | Llama-3.2 1B ppl | Llama-3.2 1B acc | Llama-2 7B ppl | Llama-2 7B acc | Llama-3 8B ppl | Llama-3 8B acc |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Dense | 9.06 | 58.3 | 5.12 | 68.4 | 5.75 | 72.9 |
| SparseGPT | 18.09 | 51.2 | 6.52 | 63.9 | 8.73 | 66.9 |
| Wanda | 21.37 | 48.5 | 6.45 | 64.0 | 9.15 | 65.4 |
| **SLaB** | **11.57** | **55.8** | **5.49** | **66.2** | **6.67** | **70.7** |

SLaB reduces perplexity by **36.04\%** vs the best baseline on Llama-3.2 1B and improves accuracy by **8.98\%**. On Llama-2 7B, SLaB (5.49) nearly matches the dense baseline (5.12).

**Higher compression ratios (unstructured, Llama-2 7B):**

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| $\mathbf{W}_{\text{S}}$ only | 32.0 | 58.2 | 53.1 | 62.0 | 49.8 |

## reproduction guide

**Compute requirements:** SLaB is training-free. The main cost is forward passes through the model for calibration data and per-layer SVD computations. A single GPU with sufficient memory to load the target model (e.g., ~14GB for Llama-2 7B in FP16) is sufficient. Runtime scales with model size and iteration count (20 iterations $\times$ number of linear layers).

**Dependencies:**
- PyTorch
- HuggingFace Transformers (for Llama model loading)
- Calibration data: 128 sequences of length 2048 from the first shard of C4

**Steps:**
1. Load the target Llama model (Llama-2 7B, Llama-3 8B, or Llama-3.2 1B).
2. Prepare calibration data: 128 random sequences of length 2048 from C4 shard 0.
3. For each linear layer (excluding first embedding and final head):
   - Forward pass to collect activation matrix $\mathbf{X}$
   - Compute $\mathbf{S}_{\text{X}} = \operatorname{diag}(\sqrt{\mathbf{X}^{\mathrm{T}}\mathbf{X}})$
   - Run Algorithm 1 for 20 iterations: alternate between updating $\mathbf{W}_{\text{B}}$ via sign, computing rank-1 SVD for $\mathbf{W}_{\text{L}}$, and pruning $\mathbf{W}_{\text{S}}$ via activation-aware magnitude thresholding
   - Replace the layer's weight with the SLaB decomposition
4. Evaluate on WikiText-2 perplexity and LM-Eval-Harness zero-shot tasks.

**No public code is available** — the paper is submitted to IEEE and no GitHub repository is linked. Reproduction requires implementing Algorithm 1 from the paper.

**Evaluation commands (estimated):**

```bash
# Perplexity evaluation
lm_eval --model hf \
  --model_args pretrained=./slab-pruned-llama2-7b \
  --tasks wikitext \
  --batch_size 8

# Zero-shot evaluation
lm_eval --model hf \
  --model_args pretrained=./slab-pruned-llama2-7b \
  --tasks arc_challenge,arc_easy,boolq,hellaswag,piqa,rte,winogrande \
  --batch_size 8
```

**Gotchas:**
- The paper uses LM-Eval-Harness v0.4.4 specifically; version differences can cause score variations.
- For semi-structured sparsity (2:4, 4:8), apply the semi-structured pruning first, then perform group-wise pruning to reach the target CR.
- The comparison group size parameter has limited impact; $(1, D_{\text{in}})$ is a safe default.
- Rank-1 is used throughout; the paper shows diminishing returns for higher ranks (Figure 3).
- The Hadamard product $\mathbf{W}_{\text{L}} \odot \mathbf{W}_{\text{B}}$ at inference time can be implemented as element-wise multiply with a binary mask (efficient on hardware with bit operations).

## notes

- The core novelty is the **binary + low-rank compensation** idea. Simply adding a low-rank residual after pruning doesn't work well (Figure 1 in the paper). The binary mask $\mathbf{W}_{\text{B}}$ captures sign/directional information that dramatically reduces the rank needed in $\mathbf{W}_{\text{L}}$, making rank-1 sufficient.
- The theoretical analysis (Lemma 1, Propositions 1 and 2) provides formal justification for: (a) why $\{+1, -1\}$ binary values are optimal, and (b) why the rank-1 initialization is a fixed-point of the alternating optimization, avoiding expensive iterative refinement.
- The method is purely one-shot — no gradient steps, no data beyond calibration, no distillation. This makes it practical for scenarios where fine-tuning is too expensive or data is unavailable.
- Results are particularly impressive at high compression: at 70\% CR on Llama-2 7B, SLaB achieves ppl 6.88 vs Wanda's 71.58 — a 10× perplexity improvement. At 80\% CR, Wanda completely collapses (ppl 3600) while SLaB remains at ppl 13.63.
- The storage cost of $\mathbf{W}_{\text{B}}$ is non-trivial: at $D_{\text{out}} \times D_{\text{in}}$ bits per layer, it accounts for the bulk of the parameter budget. The paper's CR formula shows the binary matrix costs $1/b$ of the original budget (6.25\% for FP16), meaning the "true" sparsity of $\mathbf{W}_{\text{S}}$ is less aggressive than the stated CR.
- No inference speedup benchmarks (wall-clock time, throughput) are reported. The binary matrix multiplication $\mathbf{W}_{\text{L}} \odot \mathbf{W}_{\text{B}}$ could be efficient on hardware with native bit operations (XNOR + popcount), but this is not demonstrated.
- The ablation shows that the binary component contributes most of the gain (+8.4 avg accuracy vs +0.7 from adding the low-rank on top of binary). The low-rank component's marginal contribution is small, raising the question of whether a simpler binary-only compensation could suffice.
- Only Llama-family models are tested. Generalization to other architectures (Mistral, Qwen, etc.) and non-Transformer models is not demonstrated.
- The paper is submitted to IEEE (not yet peer-reviewed at the time of arXiv posting).
