## problem

additive quantization (AQLM) enables extreme LLM compression to 2-bit with O(1) lookup-table dequantization -- zero MACs per weight group -- making it ideal for edge deployment. but at 2-bit, additive quantization catastrophically fails even with extensive beam search and PV-tuning. this paper identifies codebook initialisation as the dominant bottleneck and proposes OA-EM (output-aware expectation-maximisation), a drop-in replacement that uses Hessian-weighted Mahalanobis distance to directly minimise output reconstruction error.

## architecture

**the regime transition.** the representational ratio $\rho = N / K^M$ (weight groups per layer / codebook capacity) determines the quantization regime:

| rate | M | $K^M$ | $\rho$ (Llama 3.2 3B) | regime |
|------|---|-------|------------------------|--------|
| 3 bpp | 3 | 16.8M | ~0.07 | overcomplete ($\rho < 1$) |
| 2 bpp | 2 | 65K | ~18 | undercomplete ($\rho \gg 1$) |

at 3 bpp there are more representable points than weight groups -- initialisation errors get absorbed. at 2 bpp, the 256x capacity reduction from $K^2$ to $K$ means weight groups compete for limited codebook capacity, and initial placement becomes critical.

**OA-EM algorithm.** starting from k-means centroids, alternates for R=5 rounds:

**M-step (centroid optimisation):** minimise Hessian-weighted reconstruction error:

$$\mathcal{L}\_{\text{EM}} = \frac{1}{N} \sum\_i e\_i^T H\_i e\_i, \quad e\_i = w\_i - c\_{b\_i}$$

where $H\_i = X\_i^T X\_i + \lambda I$ (damped block-diagonal Hessian from GPTQ). centroids updated via S=10 Adam steps with cosine LR annealing from $\eta = 0.01$.

**E-step (reassignment):** Hessian-weighted Mahalanobis distance directly targeting output reconstruction:

$$b\_i = \arg\min\_k (w\_i - c\_k)^T H\_i (w\_i - c\_k)$$

this is the key difference from greedy k-means: OA-EM minimises the output reconstruction objective $\|XW - X\hat{W}\|\_F^2$ rather than weight-space Euclidean distance.

## training

| parameter | value |
|-----------|-------|
| models | Llama 3.2 3B, Llama 3.1 8B, Qwen 2.5 3B |
| calibration | 128 samples from C4 |
| EM rounds | 5 |
| adam steps per round | 10 |
| batch size | 256 |
| LR | 0.01 cosine-annealed |
| damping | $\lambda = 0.01 \cdot \text{mean}(\text{diag}(H))$ |
| beam search | b=4/8/16, e=100 epochs |
| hardware | single NVIDIA A100 |

## evaluation

**pre-PV-tuning perplexity at 2 bpp (Llama 3.2 3B):**

| init | beam=4 | beam=8 | beam=16 |
|------|--------|--------|---------|
| greedy | 352.39 | 60.61 | 46.01 |
| **OA-EM** | **16.82** | **17.39** | **16.53** |

OA-EM at beam=4 (6.1h) beats greedy at beam=16 (16.9h) -- 38% less time, 2.8x better perplexity.

**basin persistence (post-PV-tuning, Llama 3.2 3B, beam=8):**

| init | pre-PV | post-PV | gap reduction |
|------|--------|---------|---------------|
| greedy | 60.61 | 11.76 | 48.85 |
| **OA-EM** | **17.39** | **11.53** | **5.86** |

PV-tuning compresses a 43-point gap to just 0.23, yet OA-EM remains better in every configuration.

**post-PV downstream average accuracy at 2 bpp:**

| model | greedy | OA-EM |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Llama 3B (b=4) | .573 | **.589** (+1.7pp) |
| Llama 8B (b=8) | .649 | **.656** (+0.7pp) |

OA-EM wins on perplexity in every model. the LUT dequantization pathway (zero MACs) is preserved -- no inference overhead.

## reproduction guide

1. fork from https://github.com/kenno94-IK/aqlm-oaem
2. single A100, models from HuggingFace
3. quantization: 2 bpp (M=2, K=256, g=8)
4. OA-EM: 5 rounds, 10 Adam steps, bs=256, lr=0.01 cosine, lambda=0.01*mean(diag(H))
5. beam search refinement: b=4/8/16, e=100
6. PV-tuning: standard AQLM PV-tuning with straight-through estimation
7. evaluate: WikiText-2, C4 perplexity via lm-eval-harness

## notes

the representational ratio $\rho = N/K^M$ is a clean theoretical lens for understanding why extreme quantization fails. the finding that PV-tuning stays within basins (gap compresses but never vanishes) means initialisation quality matters even after fine-tuning. the asymmetric beam-width scaling -- OA-EM improves with wider beams while greedy worsens -- is strong evidence of different optimisation basins. for edge deployment, the key takeaway is that with OA-EM, AQLM at 2-bit with LUT dequantization becomes practical: 8x weight compression, zero dequant MACs, and competitive perplexity after PV-tuning (11.53 vs 7.28 FP16 on WikiText-2).
