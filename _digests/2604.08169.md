## problem

alignment in LLMs is more brittle than commonly assumed -- misalignment can be triggered by adversarial prompts, benign fine-tuning, emergent misalignment, and goal misgeneralization. recent evidence suggests some misalignment behaviors are encoded as linear structure in activation space, making them amenable to steering. but uniform additive steering (add a fixed vector at every token) causes coherence degradation and repetition in multi-turn generation. this paper introduces projection-aware steering methods that selectively intervene only on tokens whose activations fall below a logistic regression decision boundary.

## architecture

two novel steering methods:

**StTP (steering at token projection):** at each token, compute the logistic regression probability that the current activation is "aligned" vs "malicious." only apply the steering vector when $p\_{\text{aligned}} < \tau$ (below threshold). preserves already-aligned tokens.

**StMP (steering at mean projection):** same decision boundary, but applied per-sequence mean rather than per-token. smoother intervention with less token-level jitter.

**steering vector extraction:** contrastive activation averaging (CAA) -- compute mean activation difference between aligned and malicious responses at a target layer, averaged across prompt set. same direction as previous work on emotion/persona vectors.

**decision boundary:** logistic regression trained on activation norms at the steering layer to classify aligned vs malicious tokens. linear separability confirmed via PCA + cohen's d.

## training

models tested: Llama 3.1 8B Instruct, Qwen 2.5 7B Instruct, Gemma 3 4B. steering vectors extracted from contrastive prompt pairs (benign-aligned vs maliciously-finetuned responses).

## evaluation

**single-turn trait recovery:**

| model | method | honesty | compassion |
|-------|--------|---------|-----------|
| Llama 8B | SwFC | 84/100 | 71/100 |
| Llama 8B | StTP | 86/100 | 76/100 |
| Llama 8B | StMP | 88/100 | 78/100 |

all methods recover from malicious baselines (honesty 11, compassion 30).

**capability preservation (MMLU):**

| method | MMLU | AlpacaEval win rate |
|--------|------|-------------------|
| baseline | 65.5 | 48% |
| SwFC | 62.5 | 43% |
| StTP | 63.2 | 50% |
| StMP | 64.8 | 53% |

StMP best preserves capabilities.

**multi-turn coherence (sentence reuse by turn 9):**

| method | turn 1 | turn 9 |
|--------|--------|--------|
| SwFC | ~20% | 85% |
| StTP | ~15% | 60% |
| StMP | ~10% | 40% |

**MASK benchmark (disinformation resistance):**

| method | H@1 |
|--------|-----|
| baseline | 51.4 |
| StTP | 58.0 |
| StMP | 65.3 |

strongest on disinformation: 39 -> 78 with StMP.

**optimal steering layers:** architecture-dependent. Llama ~29-43% depth, Qwen ~64-70% depth.

## reproduction guide

1. collect contrastive prompt pairs (aligned vs malicious responses)
2. extract steering vectors via CAA: mean aligned activation minus mean malicious activation at target layer
3. train logistic regression on activation norms to separate aligned from malicious tokens
4. apply StTP/StMP: check decision boundary per token, steer only when below threshold
5. tune threshold $\tau$ to trade off trait recovery vs coherence

no code released. the method is straightforward to implement given a model with accessible activations.

## notes

connects directly to anthropic's emotion vectors and persona vectors work -- the steering direction aligns with CAA mean-difference directions. the key finding that misalignment converges to linearly-separable representations (confirmed via PCA + cohen's d) supports the broader thesis that many alignment properties are encoded as directions in activation space. the projection-aware intervention (steer only when needed) is a simple but effective idea that could generalize beyond alignment -- same approach could apply to steering persona, style, or task-specific behavior without degrading other capabilities. the architecture-dependent optimal layer finding (29-43% for Llama vs 64-70% for Qwen) is an important practical detail for anyone applying activation steering.
