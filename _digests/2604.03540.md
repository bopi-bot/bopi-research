## Problem

Multi-step generative policies (e.g., Diffusion Policy) achieve strong performance in robotic manipulation by modeling multimodal action distributions, but require tens to hundreds of network function evaluations (NFEs) per action for iterative denoising at inference time. This latency is prohibitive for high-frequency closed-loop control ($\geq 100\,\mathrm{Hz}$) and severely restricts online reinforcement learning. Existing one-step alternatives either (1) depend on post-hoc distillation of multi-step teacher models, inheriting a multi-step pretraining dependency, or (2) require auxiliary corrective objectives (dispersive losses, directional alignment, velocity constraints) to maintain quality under strict 1-NFE constraints. Neither delivers high policy quality as an **intrinsic** property of the training objective.

This paper proposes a "native one-step" formulation where 1-NFE inference arises directly from the training objective itself, not from post-hoc acceleration or auxiliary objective coupling. A secondary challenge is extending such a one-step backbone to online RL while preserving exact likelihood computation and the deterministic one-step deployment path.

## Architecture

The framework is **two-stage**: Stage 1 (DBP) trains a native one-step generative policy from offline demonstrations; Stage 2 (DBPO) adds an exact-likelihood stochastic adapter for PPO-style online updates.

### Generator backbone

A conditional generator $f_{\theta}: \mathbb{R}^{C} \to \mathbb{R}^{D}$ maps latent noise $\mathbf{z} \sim p_{0} = \mathcal{N}(\mathbf{0}, \mathbf{I})$ plus observation history $\mathbf{o}_{t}^{\mathrm{hist}}$ to an action chunk:

$$\hat{\mathbf{x}}_{t} = f_{\theta}(\mathbf{o}_{t}^{\mathrm{hist}}, \mathbf{z}_{t}; \tau = 0), \quad \mathbf{x}_{t} \in \mathbb{R}^{D}, \quad D = H \cdot d_{a}$$

where $H$ is the action chunk horizon and $d_{a}$ is the per-step action dimension. At deployment, a single latent code is sampled and $f_{\theta}$ is evaluated **once** (1-NFE). The generator supports heterogeneous sensory modalities: low-dimensional states, RGB images, 3D point clouds, and multi-camera inputs.

**Receding-horizon control** executes a sub-window starting at chunk index $T_{o}$ with execution length $H_{e}$: $\mathbf{x}_{t}^{\mathrm{exec}} = [\mathbf{a}_{t}^{T_{o}}, \ldots, \mathbf{a}_{t}^{T_{o}+H_{e}-1}]$.

For the point-cloud protocol, the architecture uses the **same U-Net backbone** as MP1/OMP for protocol-matched comparison. For the Diffusion Policy suite reproduction, architectures are matched to the respective baselines.

### DBPO stochastic adapter (Stage 2)

For online RL, a trainable active backbone predicts a latent-conditioned chunk mean $\boldsymbol{\mu}_{\theta}(\mathbf{o}, \mathbf{z})$ and an observation feature $\mathbf{c}_{\theta}(\mathbf{o})$. A diagonal log-std head $g_{\psi}$ is attached:

$$\pi_{\theta,\psi}(\mathbf{x} \mid \mathbf{o}, \mathbf{z}) = \mathcal{N}\!\left(\mathbf{x};\, \boldsymbol{\mu}_{\theta}(\mathbf{o}, \mathbf{z}),\, \operatorname{diag}\!\left(\boldsymbol{\sigma}_{\psi}(\mathbf{o})^{2}\right)\right)$$

During rollouts, the policy stores $(\mathbf{z}_{t}, \mathbf{x}_{t})$. Because PPO reuses the same stored $\mathbf{z}_{t}$ in evaluation epochs, this provides **exact conditional rollout likelihoods** without marginalizing over the latent variable. A frozen reference backbone ($\bar{\theta}$) provides anchor regularization.

## Training

### Stage 1: Drift-Based Policy (DBP)

DBP trains $f_{\theta}$ via **fixed-point drifting objectives** built on Drifting Models (Deng et al., 2026). The core mechanism: during training, $G$ latent hypotheses per observation interact with expert anchors through attraction-repulsion dynamics, with corrective field updates progressively absorbed into network parameters.

**Multi-scale symmetric affinity** at scales $R \in \mathcal{R}$ (default $\mathcal{R} = \{0.02, 0.05, 0.2\}$):

$$A_{i,r,u}^{(R)} = \operatorname{SymSoftmax}\!\left(-\frac{d_{i,r,u}}{R \, s_{\mathrm{norm}}}\right)$$

Balanced coefficients yield repulsion from generated/negative references ($\mathcal{I}^{-}$) and attraction toward expert positives ($\mathcal{I}^{+}$), with antisymmetric mass exchange. The aggregated multi-scale drift vector $\mathbf{V}_{i,r,:}$ produces a **fixed-point regression target**:

$$\tilde{\mathbf{X}} = \operatorname{sg}\!\left(\bar{\mathbf{G}} / s_{\mathrm{norm}} + \mathbf{V}\right)$$

$$\ell_{i} = \frac{1}{GS}\sum_{r=1}^{G}\sum_{d=1}^{S}\left(\frac{G_{i,r,d}}{s_{\mathrm{norm}}} - \tilde{X}_{i,r,d}\right)^{2}$$

The DBP loss $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{DBP}}$ equals the mean squared drift magnitude $\mathcal{D}_{\mathrm{drift}}(q_{\theta}, p; \mathcal{R})$, which under standard SGD conditions converges to a zero-drift stationary point (i.e., $q_{\theta} = p$). Two modes are supported: **chunk mode** (flatten entire action chunk, drift once) and **step-wise mode** (drift per-step slice, average over $H$).

**Hyperparameters:**
- 10 demonstrations per task
- FPS preprocessing to 512/1024 points; $84 \times 84$ image resizing
- Seeds $\{0, 1, 2\}$
- 3000 epochs (Adroit), 1000 epochs (Meta-World)
- $8 \times$ NVIDIA RTX 3090 GPUs

### Stage 2: DBPO (Online RL)

PPO with anchor regularization on the pretrained DBP backbone:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{RL}} = \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{PPO}} + \lambda_{\mathrm{anchor}} \, \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{anchor}}$$

$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{anchor}} = \mathbb{E}_{t}\!\left[\left\|\boldsymbol{\mu}_{\theta}(\mathbf{o}_{t}, \mathbf{z}_{t}) - \boldsymbol{\mu}_{\bar{\theta}}(\mathbf{o}_{t}, \mathbf{z}_{t})\right\|_{2}^{2}\right]$$

where $\bar{\theta}$ is the frozen pretrained backbone. The PPO objective $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{PPO}}$ includes the clipped surrogate $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{clip}}$, value regression $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{value}}$, and entropy bonus $\mathcal{H}$. Only the executed action prefix enters credit assignment. Exploration operates via Gaussian noise; deployment reverts to the deterministic mean.

## Evaluation

### Diffusion Policy suite (12 tasks, reproduced)

| Task | Diffusion Policy (100 NFE) | DBP (1 NFE) |
|------|---------------------------|-------------|
| Push-T (Image) | 0.91 | 0.89 |
| Push-T (Low-Dim) | 0.85 | **0.87** |
| BlockPush (P1/P2) | 0.24 | **0.43** |
| RoboMimic (Low-Dim) | 0.80 | **0.92** |
| RoboMimic (Image) | **0.91** | 0.87 |
| Kitchen (P1/P2/P3/P4) | 1.00 | 1.00 |
| **Average** | 0.79 | **0.83** |

DBP improves the family-level average from $0.79$ to $0.83$ while reducing inference from 100 NFE to 1 NFE ($100\times$ speedup).

### Online RL (DBPO)

On **RoboMimic** (4 sparse-reward image-based tasks), DBPO achieves average score **0.90** (best among compared native 1-NFE RL baselines). On **D4RL** locomotion, DBPO attains average return **4096.0**.

**Anchor ablation**: Removing $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{anchor}}$ drops performance to 0.75 (RoboMimic) and 3273.5 (D4RL), confirming anchor regularization is crucial for stabilizing online fine-tuning.

```bash
# Code (announced, may not yet be public at time of submission)

```

**Offline DBP training (point-cloud protocol, matching MP1/OMP):**
- Backbone: U-Net (same architecture as MP1/OMP)
- 10 demonstrations per task, FPS to 512/1024 points
- Image inputs resized to $84 \times 84$
- Temperature set: $\mathcal{R} = \{0.02, 0.05, 0.2\}$ (task-specific tuning may improve results)
- 3000 epochs (Adroit), 1000 epochs (Meta-World)
- Seeds: $\{0, 1, 2\}$, evaluation every 200 epochs, top-5 checkpoints averaged
- Hardware: $8 \times$ NVIDIA RTX 3090

**Online DBPO fine-tuning:**
- Initialize from pretrained DBP backbone
- Add diagonal Gaussian log-std head $g_{\psi}$ on observation features $\mathbf{c}_{\theta}(\mathbf{o})$
- PPO with anchor regularization (weight $\lambda_{\mathrm{anchor}}$)
- Frozen reference backbone $\bar{\theta}$ for anchor loss
- Store $(\mathbf{z}_{t}, \mathbf{x}_{t})$ during rollouts for exact likelihood computation
- 100 evaluation episodes per task

**Real-world deployment:**
- Dual-arm UR5 + RTX 3090
- Tri-camera: 2 wrist RealSense L515 + 1 head Orbbec Gemini
- 50 teleoperation demos per task
- Receding-horizon execution from trained DBP backbone (1-NFE, no inference modification)

## Notes

- The paper builds on **Drifting Models** (Deng et al., 2026, arXiv:2602.04770), a generative framework where refinement happens entirely at training time via evolving pushforward distributions, guaranteeing one-step inference by construction.
- Key distinction from prior work: "native one-step" means 1-NFE inference arises from the training objective itself, not from post-hoc distillation (Consistency Policy, One-Step Diffusion Policy) or auxiliary corrective terms (MP1, DM1, OMP, MVP).
- The convergence guarantee relies on SGD reaching a first-order stationary point of $\mathcal{D}_{\mathrm{drift}}$, plus additional sufficient assumptions (full-rank generator Jacobian on support of $p_{0}$, identifiable drift field: $\mathcal{V}_{p,q}(\mathbf{x}) \equiv \mathbf{0} \Rightarrow q = p$).
- Architecture is intentionally matched to MP1/OMP baselines in the point-cloud protocol to isolate algorithmic effects from architectural advantages.
- The anchor regularizer is critical: without it, online fine-tuning degrades significantly (0.75 vs 0.90 on RoboMimic, 3273.5 vs 4096.0 on D4RL), suggesting the pretrained backbone distribution is easily destabilized by reward-driven updates.
- Real-world Transport (bimanual) at 55% remains the weakest task, with failures from inter-arm synchronization conflicts and grasp slip on smooth can surfaces.
- Future work targets contact-rich long-horizon compositional tasks and improving DBPO sample efficiency under learning-from-scratch settings and larger model scales.
