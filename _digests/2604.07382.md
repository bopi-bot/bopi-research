## problem

This paper investigates whether LLMs develop internal representations of emotion that are geometrically organized in ways that parallel established psychological models---specifically the **valence--arousal model** of affect. The core question: *Do LLM latent spaces reproduce the "V"-shaped parabolic geometry of human emotion, and what does this tell us about the linear vs. nonlinear structure of representational geometry?*

**Prior work on emotion representations in LLMs:**
- Tigges et al. (2023) showed linear representations of sentiment in transformers.
- Park et al. (2023) proposed the **linear representation hypothesis**---high-level concepts encoded as simple linear directions in activation space.
- Tak & Gratch (2024) performed mechanistic interpretability of emotion inference in LLMs.
- Shah et al. (2022) demonstrated affective retrofitted word embeddings.

**Why geometric structure matters:** Emotions provide a unique testbed because their semantic organization is well-established in psychology (both categorical and dimensional), giving us an external ground truth for validating representational geometry findings---a problem that plagues most geometric analysis of LLMs. Understanding emotion representations also carries direct safety relevance (misclassification detection, selective prediction, hallucination detection).

## architecture/methods

**Core pipeline:** Extract activations from LLMs on emotion classification tasks $\to$ train pairwise logistic regression classifiers $\to$ construct dissimilarity matrices from classification accuracies $\to$ apply manifold learning (MDS / Isomap) $\to$ compare recovered geometry against psychological benchmarks.

**Geometric data analysis tools:**
1. **Classical Multidimensional Scaling (MDS)** --- Takes dissimilarity matrix $D = [d\_{ij}]$, constructs doubly-centered Gram matrix $B = -\frac{1}{2}JD^{\circ 2}J$ where $J = I - \frac{1}{n}\mathbf{1}\mathbf{1}^{\top}$, then eigendecomposes $B = Q\Lambda Q^{\top}$ to recover coordinates $z\_i \in \mathbb{R}^k$ from top-$k$ eigenpairs. Provides a **linear Euclidean** embedding.
2. **Isomap** --- Extends MDS by replacing Euclidean distances with **geodesic distance estimates** via $k$-nearest neighbor graph $G$, computing shortest-path distances $\tilde{d}\_{ij}$, then applying classical MDS on those geodesic distances. Captures **nonlinear manifold structure**.
3. **UMAP** --- Supplementary analysis; hyperparameter sweep over $n\_{\text{neighbors}} \in \{3,\dots,12\}$, selecting the value maximizing trustworthiness.
4. **Cosine-distance ablation** --- $D^{\text{cos}}\_{ij} = 1 - \cos(\bar{h}\_i, \bar{h}\_j)$ to verify findings don't depend on supervised probing.

**Key metrics:**
- Pairwise logistic regression test accuracy as dissimilarity proxy: $D\_{ij} = \text{acc}\_{ij}$ (higher accuracy = more separable = greater distance)
- Alternative mapping: $D\_{ij} = \max(0, 2 \cdot \text{acc}\_{ij} - 1)$
- **Scaled orthogonal Procrustes** $R^{2}$ for alignment with ANEW valence-arousal scores: $R^{2}\_{\text{proc}} = 1 - \frac{\|aX\_cR - Y\_c\|\_F^2}{\|Y\_c\|\_F^2}$
- Significance via label-permutation null ($T = 2000$ shuffles)
- **Trustworthiness** $T(k)$ (Venna & Kaski, 2001): measures local neighborhood preservation in low-dim embeddings, range [0, 1]
- **Participation ratio** of eigenspectrum for intrinsic dimensionality estimation
- Geodesic/Euclidean distance ratios for nonlinearity quantification

**Models evaluated:**
| Model | Params | Layers | Hidden dim | Type |
|-------|--------|--------|------------|------|

## training/experiments

**Dataset:** GoEmotions (Demszky et al., 2020) --- 58,009 English Reddit comments, 27 fine-grained emotion categories + Neutral. Restrict to single-label examples with multi-rater agreement (${\sim}83\%$ of examples). 12 positive, 11 negative, 4 ambiguous categories. For statistical tests, use the 17 of 28 classes also covered in ANEW.

**Classification pipeline:**
- Zero-shot inference using prompt: "Classify this text into exactly one emotion from this list: [shuffled emotions] Text: {text} Emotion:"
- Emotion vocabulary shuffled per prompt to reduce positional bias
- Forward hooks on every transformer block capture hidden states
- Mean pooling across sequence dimension: $\mathbf{h}\_\ell = \frac{1}{T}\sum\_{t=1}^{T}\mathbf{H}\_\ell^{(t)} \in \mathbb{R}^d$
- float16 precision, AutoModelForCausalLM

**Pairwise classification:**
- For each emotion pair $(e\_i, e\_j)$, train logistic regression with L2 regularization (max iterations = 1000) on balanced datasets with 80:20 stratified train-test split
- Test accuracy serves as dissimilarity $D\_{ij}$
- Diagonals set to zero, missing entries imputed by global mean, matrix symmetrized

**Emotion category filtering:**
- Gemma-2-9B: 20 emotions (correct samples range 58--2,212 per class)
- Mistral-7B: 16 emotions (correct samples range 29--1,293 per class)
- LLaMA-3-70B-Instruct: used for generalization test

**Layers probed:** All transformer layers for each model (42 for Gemma, 32 for Mistral, 80 for LLaMA-3-70B-Instruct). Peak separability at Gemma Layer 20, Mistral Layer 27.

**Activation separability patterns:** Mean pairwise emotion separability rises toward middle layers, slight drop at final layers. Gemma-2-9B mean test accuracies in $>0.9$ range across all layers vs. ${\sim}0.55$--${\sim}0.89$ in Mistral-7B.

## evaluation

### Valence-Arousal Alignment

**Statistical alignment test (Procrustes $R^{2}$ vs. ANEW):**
- **Gemma-2-9B:** 36 of 42 layers show statistically significant alignment ($p\_{2D} < 0.05$) with ANEW valence-arousal scores
- **Mistral-7B:** 17 of 32 layers significant (including all of the final 14 layers)
- **LLaMA-3-70B-Instruct:** 34 of 80 layers significant, concentrated among later layers
- Cosine-distance ablation: broad statistically significant alignment across all layers (robustness check)

**Qualitative:** 2D MDS embeddings show "V"-shaped parabolic layout with neutral at the vertex, positive/negative emotions diverging along two arms. Visible in virtually all Gemma layers and all but early Mistral layers.

### Linear vs. Nonlinear Structure

**Eigenspectrum analysis:** Diffuse rather than conclusively low-dimensional geometry:
- Participation ratio: Gemma-2-9B ${\sim}17$ out of 20 emotions, Mistral-7B ${\sim}8$ out of 16
- Eigenvalue decay consistent with Wigner semicircle law (random matrix baseline)

**Trustworthiness comparison (Isomap vs. classical MDS, Table 1):**

| Model | $d{=}1$ | $d{=}2$ | $d{=}3$ | $d{=}4$ | $d{=}5$ | $d{=}6$ | $d{=}7$ | $d{=}8$ |
|-------|---------|---------|---------|---------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Gemma | **+0.155** | -0.001 | -0.001 | 0.011 | 0.013 | 0.020 | 0.024 | 0.022 |
| Mistral | **+0.099** | -0.024 | -0.026 | -0.011 | -0.005 | 0.001 | -0.004 | -0.007 |

Key finding: substantial trustworthiness improvement at **rank-1** ($d{=}1$) only, where Isomap unrolls the parabolic curvature. At $d \geq 2$, differences are negligible ($<0.03$). This means the only meaningful nonlinearity is the parabolic bending in rank-1.

**Geodesic vs. Euclidean distance ratios:**
- Gemma-2-9B: 1.00 to 1.80 (10th--90th percentile)
- Mistral-7B: 0.80 to 1.42

**Interpretation:** LLM emotion spaces are "almost Euclidean" --- high-rank, diffuse, lacking strong low-dimensional curvature. The nonlinearity that exists is consistent with the natural parabolic bending of valence--arousal space. Locally amenable to linear analysis, but global structure is nonlinear.

### Uncertainty Quantification

**Key finding:** Misclassified activations lie closer to pairwise separating hyperplanes than correct activations (not tautological: evaluated only on out-of-sample test data).

**UQ model performance:**

| Model | Accuracy | AUC-ROC | Baseline Acc. | ECE |
|-------|----------|---------|---------------|-----|
| Gemma-2-9B | **77.6%** | **0.813** | 69.4% | **0.011** |
| Mistral-7B | **85.7%** | **0.871** | 82.2% | **0.007** |
| LLaMA-3-70B-Instruct | **80.1%** | **0.822** | 75.6% | **0.011** |

- Gemma: 180 binary problems (3,858 correct vs. 8,748 misclassified), 60:20:20 split
- Mistral: 122 binary problems (5,254 correct vs. 15,216 misclassified)
- Low ECE means: predicted probability of 0.8 $\approx$ 80% empirical correctness rate

### Steering Experiments (Appendix D)

On LLaMA-3-70B-Instruct, probe-derived directions used as causal activation steering vectors:

| Condition | Coherence (SEM) | Valence (SEM) |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| Direct positive (joy) | 8.68 (0.33) | **7.88** (0.38) |
| Neutral-first positive | 8.22 (0.38) | **6.70** (0.46) |
| Direct negative (anger) | 2.47 (0.23) | **1.07** (0.03) |
| Neutral-first negative | 2.40 (0.15) | **1.08** (0.05) |

Valence separation: $>6$ points on 10-point scale (7.29 vs. 1.08 pooled). Inter-rater reliability: ICC = 0.953 (valence), 0.858 (coherence). Anger steering causes near-distributional collapse (${~}40\%$ expletive tokens). Joy steering preserves fluency.

## reproduction guide

**Steps to reproduce:**
1. Load GoEmotions dataset; filter to single-label examples with multi-rater agreement
2. Run zero-shot emotion classification on target LLM with shuffled emotion vocabulary
3. Extract mean-pooled hidden states via forward hooks on all transformer layers
4. For each emotion pair, train balanced pairwise logistic regression (L2, max\_iter=1000, 80:20 split)
5. Build dissimilarity matrix from test accuracies; apply classical MDS and Isomap
6. Run Procrustes alignment test against ANEW valence-arousal coordinates ($T = 2000$ permutations)
7. Compute trustworthiness, participation ratio, geodesic/Euclidean distance ratios
8. For UQ: train second-round logistic regression on hyperplane distances

**Compute requirements:**
- NVIDIA H200 GPUs
- PyTorch 2.5.1, CUDA 12.1
- LLaMA-3-70B-Instruct inference requires ${\sim}140$GB VRAM at float16 (multi-GPU)
- 7B/9B models run on single GPU
- No training of LLMs required (all zero-shot)

**Dependencies:** PyTorch, scikit-learn (logistic regression, MDS), scipy (Isomap via $k$NN graph + shortest paths), umap-learn, numpy, datasets (HuggingFace for GoEmotions), transformers (AutoModelForCausalLM)

## notes

**Connection to Anthropic emotion/persona vectors work:**
This paper provides strong complementary evidence for the Anthropic emotion vectors and persona vectors research program. While Anthropic's work focuses on discovering and manipulating single-direction emotion vectors (activation steering), this paper provides the **geometric characterization** of why such single directions exist: the latent space is organized along a parabolic valence--arousal manifold where individual emotions are roughly linearly separable. The steering experiments in Appendix D directly validate the Anthropic paradigm---probe-derived directions between joy and anger causally shift generated text valence by $>6$ points on a 10-point scale.

**Linear representation hypothesis:**
The paper provides **nuanced support**: affective geometry is "locally amenable to linear analysis" (trustworthiness differences $< 0.03$ at $d \geq 2$), but the global rank-1 structure is **nonlinear** (parabolic curvature confirmed by Isomap trustworthiness gains of +0.155 for Gemma, +0.099 for Mistral at $d{=}1$). This suggests the linear representation hypothesis is a good **local approximation** but insufficient as a complete description. The high participation ratios (${\sim}17$ for Gemma) and diffuse eigenspectra suggest the space is high-dimensional, not a simple 2D plane.

**Key tensions with prior findings:**
- The parabolic "V"-shape contradicts the earlier **circumplex model** (Russell, 1980) and supports the newer **parabolic model** (Kim et al., 2020; Maleki et al., 2023) where neutral is at the vertex rather than the center.
- The near-Euclidean nature of the space (geodesic/Euclidean ratios near 1.0) contrasts with claims of strongly curved or simplex-like structures in other semantic domains (Park et al., 2024 on categorical concepts).
- The asymmetry in steering (joy preserves fluency, anger causes collapse) suggests RLHF/instruction-tuning has **attenuated negative-affect representations**, creating an off-manifold problem for negative steering.
- The parallel with human EEG data (FACED dataset, Appendix B)---showing the same parabolic "V"-shape in neural activity---is suggestive but preliminary (single dataset, 123 subjects, no causal claim).

**Practical implications for Binh's research:**
- The MDS+Procrustes pipeline offers a principled way to validate that emotion vectors discovered in any model align with human psychological models
- The UQ framework (hyperplane distance $\to$ calibrated probability) could be applied to detect when emotion steering is going off-manifold
- The neutral-first routing strategy, while inconclusive for coherence rescue, provides a geometrically-motivated approach to composing steering vectors along manifold geodesics rather than straight lines
