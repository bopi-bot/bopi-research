## Problem

Neural network *grokking* — the abrupt memorization-to-generalization transition first reported by Power et al. (2022) — defies standard learning theory: a network that already fits training data perfectly later undergoes a sharp jump in test accuracy. Existing explanations (circuit formation, representation learning, circuit efficiency, first-order phase transitions) remain qualitative. This paper asks whether grokking can be rigorously characterized as a **dimensional phase transition** governed by **self-organized criticality (SOC)**, using finite-size scaling (FSS) of gradient avalanche dynamics across multiple model scales.

## Architecture

The primary experimental testbed is an **XOR boolean function** (4 samples, 2 inputs → 1 output) trained on a **multilayer perceptron**:

$$\text{Input}[2] \to \text{Hidden}[h] \to \text{Output}[1], \quad N = 4h + 1 \text{ parameters}$$

- **Eight model scales:** $h \in \{20, 30, 50, 70, 100, 120, 200, 500\}$, spanning $N = 81$ to $N = 2001$ parameters (1.4 decades).
- **Diffusion graph:** All trainable parameters are concatenated and mapped onto a **Barabási–Albert scale-free network** ($m=2$, $\langle k \rangle = 4$) for the threshold-based diffusion probe.
- **Loss:** Binary cross-entropy.

A **companion study** (Wang 2026, submitted to APS Open Science) validates the same $D(t)$ signature on a **Transformer** trained on **ModAdd-59** (80/20 train/test split, ~120k parameters — ~60× the largest XOR scale), establishing generality beyond the minimal XOR setting.

**Note on XOR limitation:** XOR lacks a separate test split (training and evaluation use the same four patterns). The paper argues this is a *methodological feature* — it isolates the gradient-level phase transition from behavioral confounds — and relies on the companion study for canonical grokking confirmation.

## Training

### Setup

- **Optimizer:** SGD with learning rate $\eta = 0.5$, **500 epochs**.
- **Seeds:** 6 independent seeds per scale; 1000 seeds for weight concentration (Gini) validation.
- **Gradient snapshots:** 51 snapshots recorded at 10-epoch intervals.
- **Grokking timing:** Scale-dependent, spanning epochs 12–134 across $h = 20$–$500$. Representative single-scale grokking at $h = 21$, $N = 85$: abrupt accuracy jump at epoch 27.

### TDU-OFC Probe: Threshold-based Diffusion Update (Olami–Feder–Christensen)

Real training gradients are injected as initial conditions into a threshold-driven diffusion process inspired by the OFC earthquake model:

1. **Threshold:** $\tau = Q_{90}(\lvert \nabla L \rvert)$ — 90th percentile of gradient magnitudes, computed per epoch (robust across $Q_{80}$–$Q_{95}$).
2. **Diffusion rule** — for nodes $i$ with $\lvert g_i \rvert > \tau$:
$$g_i' = (1 - \alpha)\, g_i, \quad g_j' = g_j + \frac{\alpha \, g_i}{k_i} \;\;\text{for all } j \sim i$$
   where $k_i$ is node degree, $\alpha = 0.3$ (diffusion strength, robust across $\alpha \in [0.1, 0.5]$, CV $< 0.4\%$), and $j \sim i$ denotes graph neighbors.
3. **Iteration:** Max 20 steps (real training typically $< 10$) until no nodes exceed threshold.
4. **Avalanche size** $s$: total triggered updates — measures how far gradient perturbations propagate through the parameter graph.

### Key Findings from $D(t)$ Evolution

The effective dimensionality $D$ is extracted via FSS: $s_{\max} \sim N^{D}$ across system sizes $N$. Three lines of evidence:

1. **Time-resolved evolution:** $D(t)$ evolves continuously from $D \approx 0.90$ (sub-diffusive, pre-grokking) through the random-diffusion baseline $D \approx 1$ to $D \approx 1.20$ (super-diffusive, post-grokking) — a **30% dynamic range**.

2. **Aggregate scaling:** Heavy-tailed CCDFs collapse across eight scales with $D = 1.00 \pm 0.02$ ($R^2 = 1.00$) and mean avalanche exponent $\gamma = 1.15 \pm 0.06$ ($R^2 = 0.99$).

3. **Phase-resolved bootstrap** (10,000 resamples, each run split at its own grokking epoch):
   - Pre-grokking: $D_{\mathrm{pre}} = 0.90 \pm 0.02$
   - Post-grokking: $D_{\mathrm{post}} = 1.20 \pm 0.02$
   - Synthetic i.i.d. Gaussian baseline: $D_{\mathrm{synth}} = 0.99 \pm 0.01$
   - Three **non-overlapping** peaks confirm statistically distinct scaling regimes.

## Evaluation

### Effective Dimensionality Exponents

| Regime | $D$ value | Interpretation |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| Pre-grokking (real gradients) | $0.90 \pm 0.02$ | Sub-diffusive (spatially confined cascades) |
| Random baseline (synthetic) | $0.99 \pm 0.01$ | i.i.d. Gaussian gradients, topology-invariant |
| Post-grokking (real gradients) | $1.20 \pm 0.02$ | Super-diffusive (collectively amplified cascades) |
| Aggregate (all training) | $1.00 \pm 0.02$ | Time-average of two distinct regimes |
| Mean avalanche exponent $\gamma$ | $1.15 \pm 0.06$ | $\langle s \rangle \sim N^{\gamma}$, $R^2 = 0.99$ |

### Topology Invariance (Control Experiment)

Synthetic i.i.d. Gaussian gradients across **30 configurations** (5 network topologies × 6 seeds) — including 1D rings to random graphs — all collapse to $D \approx 0.99$ with coefficient of variation $\mathrm{CV} < 0.3\%$. This invariance persists across $\alpha = 0.1$–$0.5$ ($\mathrm{CV} \lesssim 1\%$), confirming $D$ reflects **gradient field geometry**, not network architecture.

### Weight Concentration Signature

Gini coefficient of $\lvert \bm{\theta} \rvert$ exhibits a transient **+25% peak** lasting ~50 epochs at generalization transition. Validated across 1000 seeds: peak timing synchronizes with grokking within $\pm 10$ epochs.

### FSS Fit Quality

- Data collapse of CCDFs using single exponent $D$ across eight scales ($R^2 > 0.98$ per time slice).
- Leave-one-out FSS analysis: removing any single scale preserves $D$, confirming internal self-consistency.

### Cross-Task Validation (Companion Study)

Identical $D(t)$ crossing signature confirmed on **ModAdd-59** with a Transformer (80/20 split), establishing that the gradient mechanism generalizes to classical grokking. Ungrokked-run negative controls confirm criticality is grokking-specific.

## Reproduction Guide

### Minimal Setup (XOR)

```
# Architecture: MLP(2 -> h -> 1), N = 4h + 1 params
# Scales: h in {20, 30, 50, 70, 100, 120, 200, 500}
# Loss: binary cross-entropy
# Optimizer: SGD, lr=0.5, 500 epochs
# Dataset: XOR (4 samples, 2 inputs, 1 output)
# Seeds: 6 per scale (51 gradient snapshots at 10-epoch intervals)

# TDU-OFC Probe
# 1. Map all parameters to Barabasi-Albert graph (m=2, <k>=4)
# 2. Per epoch: threshold tau = Q90(|grad L|)
# 3. Diffusion: alpha=0.3, max 20 steps
# 4. Record avalanche sizes s
# 5. FSS: fit s_max ~ N^D across 8 scales
```

### Key Hyperparameters

| Parameter | Value | Robustness |
|-----------|-------|------------|
| $\eta$ (learning rate) | 0.5 | — |
| $\alpha$ (diffusion strength) | 0.3 | $\alpha \in [0.1, 0.5]$, CV $< 0.4\%$ |
| Threshold percentile | $Q_{90}$ | $Q_{80}$–$Q_{95}$ robust |
| Diffusion max steps | 20 | Real training $< 10$ |
| BA graph $m$ | 2 | — |

### Critical Dependencies

- FSS requires **systematic variation of system size** (analogous to lattice sizes in Ising models) — at least 5+ scales spanning >1 decade.
- Phase-splitting at each run's own grokking epoch is essential for resolving the two distinct $D$ regimes.
- Companion study code for Transformer/ModAdd-59 validation referenced but not linked.

## Notes

**Core claim:** Grokking is a *dimensional phase transition* in gradient space — the effective dimensionality $D$ (quantifying how avalanche extent scales with system size) crosses from sub-diffusive ($D < 1$) to super-diffusive ($D > 1$) at generalization onset. This is framed as **self-organized criticality**: the system self-tunes to a critical manifold without external parameter tuning.

**Strengths:**
- Rigorous FSS methodology across 8 scales (1.4 decades), with bootstrap confidence intervals and leave-one-out validation.
- Clean experimental design: topology invariance control ($\mathrm{CV} < 0.3\%$) convincingly rules out architectural confounds.
- The quasi-1D cascade geometry ($D \approx 1$) is a novel empirical finding that connects gradient correlations to low-dimensional solution manifolds (Gur-Ari et al. 2018).
- Shadow-probe controls in the companion study ($\alpha_{\mathrm{train}} = 0$, diffusion excluded from training) confirm $D(t)$ crossing is not a probe artifact.

**Limitations:**
- The XOR testbed has no train/test split — the "grokking" observed is an abrupt learning transition in gradient geometry, not canonical delayed generalization. The paper relies entirely on a **not-yet-published companion study** for the classical grokking setting.
- No code release linked; reproduction requires re-implementing the TDU-OFC probe.
- The quasi-1D geometry ($\gamma = 1.15$) is distinct from 2D sandpile-type SOC — the paper acknowledges the **universality class remains an open question**.
- The practical implication (gradient preprocessing / optimizer design over architectural choices) is speculative without experiments on modern architectures.
- Redistributed gradient vector deviates ~30° from original in parameter space — while $D$ is topology-invariant, the probe's local modifications are substantial.

**Connection to prior work:** Complements Ghavasieh et al. (2025) on quasi-critical avalanche dynamics and tunable universality classes — those probe signal propagation criticality while this work measures gradient dynamics criticality during training.
