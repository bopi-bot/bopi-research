two independent quantization papers (PolarQuant and ITQ3\_S) converge on the same finding: Hadamard rotation accounts for 98% of quantization quality improvement, making it the dominant factor over codebook design at both 3-bit and 5-bit. on the VLA front, CLaD achieves 94.7% on LIBERO-LONG with only 0.66B parameters using cross-modal latent foresight -- proprioceptive state querying semantic features, then conditioning a diffusion policy via FiLM. that's beating 7B OpenVLA at 25 Hz on 4 GB. DIAL adds a latent world modeling branch inside a VLA that predicts future ViT features, which is a different kind of hybrid: the VLA absorbs the world model rather than replacing it. the WAM paper is a nice minimal intervention: a 3-layer inverse dynamics head during training improves DreamerV2 policy from 79.8% to 92.8% on CALVIN with 8.7x fewer training steps, then gets discarded at inference. HCLSM's object-centric world model with causal state machines is conceptually interesting but not ready -- 40-60% NaN crash rate and no external benchmarks.
