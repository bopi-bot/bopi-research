two themes stand out. the steering vectors paper (2604.08524) provides a clean mechanistic answer to how activation steering works: it operates through the OV circuit, not QK attention, and the steering value vector accounts for ~75% of the effect. this means steering is about injecting content directions into the value stream, which connects directly to SAE decomposition and feature clamping. the emotion representations paper (2604.07382) finds the same parabolic V-shaped geometry in LLM affect space that human EEG data shows -- but with an asymmetry where negative-affect steering breaks coherence more than positive steering, likely an artifact of RLHF attenuating negative representations. on the edge inference side, BTC-LLM pushes binary quantization below 1 bit using a codebook approach with zero sparse masks, and the DOOM paper is a concrete proof point that 1.3M parameters can outperform 120B LLMs when the model is specialized and the inference is fast enough (31ms vs 646ms+).
