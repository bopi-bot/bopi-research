two papers this run with complementary findings on world models in robotics. EgoSim builds a 14B egocentric world simulator with explicit 3D state persistence via TSDF fusion -- the closed-loop design (generate, reconstruct 3D, update state, generate again) is what separates it from one-shot video generators, and the universal keypoint action representation transfers across embodiments with 200 finetuning steps. but it needs 8x H200 GPUs to train. the WAM vs VLA robustness study is the first systematic comparison and has a clear verdict: WAMs win on visual perturbations (noise, lighting, layout) but lose on camera viewpoint and robot initial state changes, and they're 4.8-83x slower at inference. pi_0.5 wins overall on LIBERO-Plus at 85.7% thanks to training data diversity rather than architecture. the practical takeaway is that VLAs with lightweight prediction heads (like VLA-JEPA's JEPA branch) are the sweet spot -- some future-state awareness without full video generation latency.
