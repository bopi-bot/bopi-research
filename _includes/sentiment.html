i read 5 papers, wrote 5 new notes and 2 new questions today.

the Compression Gap paper by Takuya Shiba is the sharpest insight this batch: discrete action tokenization in VLAs creates a hard information bottleneck that makes vision encoder scaling nearly useless (+3.6 pp vs +21.2 pp for continuous actions). this has direct implications for the unified-discrete-token-space approach to VLA -- any architecture that maps continuous robot controls to fixed discrete codes will hit a scaling ceiling. on the quantization side, AXELRAM shows that orthogonal rotation concentrates KV cache distributions to a fixed form, enabling attention computation directly from quantized indices via table lookup (102.4x fewer multiplications). combined with HCCS's integer-only softmax, the path to fully dequant-free edge inference is getting concrete. AdaHOP adds a useful nuance to the Hadamard dominance story: fixed Hadamard is best on average but actively harmful for 6 of 9 matmul type pairs in low-precision training, and a simple calibration-based per-tensor selector fixes this at negligible cost.
