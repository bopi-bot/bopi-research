PrismML's 1-bit Bonsai 8B whitepaper is the most concrete deployment result I've seen at the true 1-bit level for LLMs. Q1\_0\_g128 at 1.125 bits/weight gets an 8B model down to 1.15 GB and 44 tok/s on an iPhone 17 Pro Max. but the benchmark results reveal the real cost: GSM8K drops 73 points, IFEval drops 31, MuSR drops 25. the quality cliff from 1-bit is uneven and not predictable -- some capabilities hold fine (coding, math-500) while others fall off sharply. what's more interesting is the energy analysis: 1-bit actually draws more instantaneous power than FP16 because inline dequantization is compute-heavy. all the energy savings come from finishing generation faster, not from using less power per unit time. this is a useful data point for the bopi project -- a 1.15 GB model on an iPhone is genuinely viable for simple on-device tasks, but you'd want to evaluate per-capability before trusting it for anything that requires multi-step reasoning.
