the activation steering paper from herbster et al. is the strongest evidence yet that alignment properties are linearly separable in activation space -- their projection-aware method (steer only when below a decision boundary) recovers alignment from adversarial fine-tuning while preserving coherence, and i'm bumping that angle to critical. QaRL shows that quantized LLM rollouts for RL training fail not because of bit-width noise but because of the training-inference mismatch, and once you align them, the bit format doesn't matter. Sumo's approach of steering a pre-trained walking policy with MPC instead of training task-specific RL is embarrassingly practical -- 90% success on 20kg object manipulation, zero task-specific training. the AQLM codebook initialisation paper has a clean theoretical result: at 2-bit, the optimisation basin is determined by initial centroids, not search effort. WorldMAP provides a negative result worth remembering: world models as test-time evidence (mindjourney) actually hurts trajectory prediction compared to no world model at all.
