MMaDA-VLA generates future goal observations alongside action chunks in a unified diffusion framework, achieving 98% on LIBERO without any separate world model. LaMP uses 3D scene flow as a latent motion prior. AirVLA transfers $\pi\_{0}$ to aerial manipulation via inference-time guidance, showing the base VLA captures enough dynamics to work across embodiments with targeted interventions. FODMP replaces multi-step diffusion with one-step consistency distillation while preserving temporal motion structure, making VLAs fast enough for dynamic tasks like ball catching. DFM-VLA (2603.26320) achieves 95.7% on LIBERO and 70.8% on real bimanual tasks using discrete flow matching for iterative action refinement -- no world model, no vision generation, just better action decoding. generative sim-to-real RL (2603.18532) shows RL fine-tuning VLAs directly in diverse simulated 3D worlds transfers to real robots at 75% success, without any explicit world model component. R3DP (2603.14498) demonstrates that injecting 3D foundation model priors via fast-slow feature propagation into a diffusion policy achieves 69% on RoboTwin vs 59.9% for $\pi\_{0}$, purely through better perception architecture. DIAL (2603.29844) adds a latent world modeling branch inside a VLA that predicts future ViT features -- a hybrid where the VLA absorbs world model functionality internally rather than keeping it separate, achieving 70.2% on RoboCasa GR1 (24 tasks). CLaD (2603.29409) goes further with only 0.66B parameters: cross-modal latent foresight conditions a diffusion policy on predicted future states, hitting 94.7% on LIBERO-LONG and beating 7B OpenVLA at 25 Hz inference. the WAM vs VLA robustness study (2603.22078) adds nuance: WAMs (Cosmos-Policy 82.2%, GE-Act 80.3% on LIBERO-Plus) beat pure VLAs on visual perturbations (noise, lighting, layout) thanks to spatiotemporal video priors, but $\pi\_{0.5$ still wins overall at 85.7% thanks to diverse training data. critically, WAMs are 4.8-83$\times$ slower at inference, making them impractical for real-time control. hybrid VLA-JEPA (77.9%) sits between -- getting some robustness from future prediction without full latency cost. the answer is becoming clear: VLAs win on speed and training-data-driven robustness, WAMs win on visual robustness from video priors. the practical path is VLAs with lightweight prediction heads (like VLA-JEPA's JEPA branch) rather than full video generation.
